3 Potential Investment Opportunities in Ghana Now

Jay K. Sep 26, 2024 · 5 min read

Dear potential investor, have you ever wondered where to begin your investments and are at a loss? Maybe you do not want to venture into the traditional investment setups like Treasury bills, stocks, bonds etc?

Look no further, I have prepared for you three investment opportunities you may or may have not heard about which has great potential to provide massive returns.

Potential here means that these are areas that many people have not discovered yet, or are in the known but do not accord any much importance. These are the areas I bring to your notice. So read on to know more.

But before that, let us take a good look at what it takes to be a successful investor in this current Ghana.

Characteristics of A Successful Investor

  • Diversifies portfolio across various asset classes to shoulder unforseen risks.
  • Disciplined and patient in managing investments over time.
  • Stays informed about market trends and emerging investment opportunities.
  • Embraces technology and data analytics for smarter decision-making.

Now let us look at 3 potential invesment opportunities in Ghana now.

Potential No. 1

Plot of Land Acquisition

Initial capital required: GHS2000 - GHS10000
Land acquisition, even though it has been around for many years, is not seen as an investment opportunity since many people just acquire lands for the purpose of building on them. What I want you to know is that, land can also be acquired as an investment. Take a look at this real-life scenario: a land bought for GHS200 in 2014 (about 10 years ago) now commands a price of GHS15000 in 2024. This is an outstanding return on a piece of investment. There is an adage that says "land appreciates but not depreciate". So if you can afford, go ahead, acquire plots of land even if its in the outskirts of town, hold on for years and see the wonders of land acquisition investment.

Warning
Land acquisition in Ghana generally requires thorough research. Many people have been defrauded of huge sums of monies so you need to be very careful. Always make sure the necessary documentation are legal and valid before committing any money.


Potential No. 2

Diversification of Portfolios

The Bible implicitly supports the concept of diversification in Ecclesiastes 11:2, which says, *"Give a portion to seven, or even to eight, for you know not what disaster may happen on earth."* This can be interpreted as a reminder not to put all your eggs in one basket, as spreading investments across different areas can protect against unforeseen losses.

Words of Wisdom:

Spread your investments across multiple avenues as insurance against the future unknown.



NOTABLE MENTIONS


The Warning To Avoiding Greed

The Bible warns against the dangers of greed as a way to grow wealth. In 1 Timothy 6:10, the Bible warns, "For the love of money is a root of all kinds of evil." Christians are encouraged to keep their motivations pure and avoid placing their trust in wealth. Proverbs 23:4-5 also reminds us not to overwork ourselves to gain wealth, saying, "Do not wear yourself out to get rich; do not trust your own cleverness." Wealth can be fleeting, and it is important not to make it an idol.

Words of Wisdom:

Guard yourself against unrealistic promises of substantial returns from little investments


Generosity

One of the core values of biblical financial management is generosity. Proverbs 11:25 says, *"A generous person will prosper; whoever refreshes others will be refreshed."* The Bible encourages us to use our resources, including our investments, to bless others and support the work of God. Acts 20:35 also reminds us that "It is more blessed to give than to receive."

Words of Wisdom:

Make it your goal to give as your investments return profits.


All said and done...

Judging from the above principles, the Bible does not condemn investments but rather approves it. The Bible rathers calls Christians to be wise stewards of their resources, managing them with diligence, generosity, and a heart that seeks to glorify God. By following biblical principles such as stewardship, careful planning, and generosity, investments can be a tool not only for personal financial growth but for advancing God’s kingdom and serving others.
